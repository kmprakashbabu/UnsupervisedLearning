#Finding optimal no. of clusters
clusters=range(1,10)
meanDistortions=[]

#Run the model for different cluster size to draw elbow chart to find optimum number of clusters
for k in clusters:
    model=KMeans(n_clusters=k)
    model.fit(df_std_copy)
    prediction=model.predict(df_std_copy)
    meanDistortions.append(sum(np.min(cdist(df_std_copy, model.cluster_centers_, 'euclidean'), axis=1)) / df_std_copy
                          .shape[0])



plt.plot(clusters, meanDistortions, 'bx-')
plt.xlabel('k')
plt.ylabel('Average distortion')
plt.title('Selecting k with the Elbow Method')

From the above chart we can conclude that we can form 6 clusters. Next we can run the dendogram to find out if 6 clusters yield better clustring. If not we can reduce the numbers or atleast match to KMeans

Analysis¶

We can segment the customers in to 3 categories.

  1. Customers who like to bank in person
  2. Customers who like to bank over phone
  3. Customers who like to bank online

If we look at the boxplot and original data we can deduce following

  1. Customers with higher credit limit have more number of credit cards
  2. Customers who do banking online have higher credit limit
  3. Customers who do banking over phone are next to online customers and they have credit limit less than online customers but more than customer who do banking in person
  4. Last segment of customers who do banking in person have low credit score and less credit cards

Based on the findings, the bank can improve their online services and customer experience to target new and existing customers to sell their products.
